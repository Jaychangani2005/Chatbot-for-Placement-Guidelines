**# CHARUSAT Placement Guidelines for Students (2026–27 Batch)**



**Charotar University of Science and Technology (CHARUSAT) is committed to producing graduates with strong employability skills. To achieve this objective, the University has established a centralized Career Development and Placement Cell (CDPC) to serve students across all programmes.**



**One of the primary responsibilities of the CDPC is to facilitate campus placement opportunities for eligible students. These Placement Guidelines define the rules, responsibilities, and procedures governing the campus placement process at CHARUSAT.**



**The CDPC approaches various organizations for campus placements. These may be categorized as CDPC-facilitated placements or self-sourced placements. The CDPC provides assistance to eligible students at every stage of the selection process. However, final placement outcomes depend on factors such as economic conditions, student performance, external factors, eligibility criteria, and students' technical and soft skills.**



**---**



**## CDPC-Facilitated Placement Drive**



**Companies visiting the campus (in-person or virtually) fall under this category. All such drives will be considered CDPC-facilitated placements wherever student data has been submitted through the CDPC (centrally or at the institute/department level).**



**Students may also appear directly (in-person or virtually) as per company decisions; however, such cases will still be treated under this category. The CDPC will support students at every stage up to joining.**



**---**



**## Self-Sourced Placement Drive**



**Students applying to companies not associated with CHARUSAT and without CDPC support fall under this category. In such cases, CDPC may provide limited assistance (such as interview preparation) but will not be responsible for further support.**



**\*\*Note:\*\* Self-sourced job offers of a student already placed through CDPC will be considered only if the offered package is at least 5 LPA higher than the existing offer.**



**---**



**## Pool Campus Drive**



**Students participating in placement drives conducted at other institutes (host institutes), where multiple colleges are invited, fall under this category. These drives may be conducted in-person or virtually and may be facilitated through CDPC or at the institute/department level.**



**The placement will be counted under the pool campus category provided the student’s data is shared with CDPC. Necessary support will be extended by CDPC up to the joining stage.**



**---**



**## Eligibility Criteria for Campus Placement**



**Students must meet the following criteria to participate in placement drives:**



**- Students must demonstrate a clear intention to pursue placements. Those opting for higher studies, entrepreneurship, or other career paths should not participate in campus placements.**

**- A minimum of 80% attendance in placement-related activities is required.**

**- Attendance at Pre-Placement Talks (PPTs) is mandatory.**

**- Participation in domain-related technical platforms is compulsory.**

**- Students must complete at least two certification courses:**

&#x20; **- One from NPTEL**

&#x20; **- One in their respective domain (validated by faculty TPRs)**

**- Students must maintain an updated and professional LinkedIn profile.**

**- Students must qualify for minimum cut-off marks in:**

&#x20; **- Aptitude tests conducted by CDPC**

&#x20; **- Domain-specific tests conducted by the institute/TPRs**



**---**



**## Preparation for Placement**



**CDPC will conduct structured training programmes to enhance student employability, including:**



**- Aptitude training**  

**- Technical skill development sessions**  

**- Soft skills and communication workshops**  

**- Domain knowledge sessions by industry experts**  

**- Mock interviews**  



**Attendance in training programmes conducted by the Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences (FOHSS) is mandatory.**



**---**



**## Post-Placement Policy**



**- Companies are expected to declare results on the same day, and CDPC will communicate them promptly.**

**- The University follows a strict \*\*“one student, one job”\*\* policy.**

**- If multiple offers are received on the same day, the student may choose one.**

**- Delayed offers (after accepting another) will be considered invalid.**



**---**



**## General Rules and Regulations**



**- Students must wear formal attire and shoes during recruitment.**

**- ID cards must be carried at all times.**

**- Students must report at least 15 minutes before the scheduled time.**

**- Students must carry two copies of their updated resume and required documents.**

**- Students must stay informed through official communication channels (email, WhatsApp, CDPC updates).**

**- Queries should be directed to departmental placement representatives.**

**- Misconduct, impersonation, or malpractice will result in disqualification.**

**- If a student registers but does not appear without informing CDPC, they may be treated as placed.**

**- Students must complete the entire selection process once they apply.**

**- Refusal to accept an offer without valid approval will result in strict action.**

**- Students must not apply off-campus to companies visiting through CDPC without prior approval.**

**- Directly contacting company personnel during campus drives is strictly prohibited.**



**---**



**## Non-Compliance**



**- Students not following dress code will not be allowed to participate.**

**- Misconduct or lack of coordination may lead to restrictions from future drives.**

**- Cheating or impersonation will result in complete debarment from placements.**

**- Students who do not join after accepting an offer will be permanently barred.**

**- Serious violations may lead to disciplinary action by the institute/university.**

**- In case of violation, a penalty of INR 25,000 may be deposited in the CHARUSAT account after confirmation.**
